I previously worked as co-founder and CEO of my own startup Shortcut Robotics. At Shortcut, we produced video call (telepresence) robots that users could call into and then control remotely. I entered into the project with minimal technical experience, but my time at Shortcut (~ 3 years), I picked up several software and technical skills. By the end I had designed and printed 3D parts, written code in 6 different languages and was able to fully construct a robot from just raw materials. I sampled many job functions as an early-stage startup founder, but over time I ended up gravitating toward the coding side of the business. I found coding to be very rewarding white allowing me to frequently utilize my creativity and problem-solving skills.
Ultimately, I decided I wanted to pursue software engineering as a full time profession and decided a software bootcamp (App Academy) was right for me. While I did a lot of coding during my startup, I essentially learned exactly what I needed for our specific application and not much more. During the bootcamp, I learned to round out that knowledge base and now feel fundamentally solid as a software engineer. I also gained proficiency in various languages like JavaScript, Python, CSS and HTML and various technologies like React, Redux, Flask, Websockets and AWS. I also worked on several projects, of which I’m most proud of a clone of the website Trello in which I implemented Atlassian’s drag and drop library for seamless task organization. Today, I’m looking forward to entering my first software engineering role.
